WELCOME!

Hello—I'm Gareth! I'm a mapmaker, fiction writer, educator, and currently a PhD student at the University of Wisconsin-Madison. My research interests include atlases, place-based storytelling, situated learning, and indigenous cartographies. Much of my mapmaking has manifested as stories, tours, and atlases, and in particular the use and creation of open-source mapmaking tools. I believe that maps can be used to challenge and expand our understandings of places and broader power structures, and I hope to inspire my students to do so as well. Check out my resume if you think our interests might align. I'd love to hear from you at baldricafran@wisc.edu.

You can also find source code for most of my digital projects on my Github page.


MAPS

Mapping Teejop

A series of walking tours of the University of Wisconsin—Madison campus designed to highlight indigenous history and presence. Made in partnership with Drs. Kasey Keeler and Sasha Suarez.

Globes that Maybe Were

A visual repository of the wonderous and absurd world of globe patents. Inspired by Patents and Cartographic Inventions by Mark Monmonier.

Coastal Erosion Viewer

A tool for exploring erosion on Wisconsin's Lake Michigan coast. Made in partnership with David Hart and Wisconsin Sea Grant.

Global Madison

An educational walking tour through downtown Madison, Wisconsin, highlighting the city's changing relationship to globalization over time. Created in partnership with Drs. Stephen Young, Sarah Moore, and Robert Roth.

Where the Naiads Go

The visual story of freshwater mussels in the Mississippi basin, emphasizing their role in oral tradition, environmental health, and industry.

Pumpkin Patch

An experiment in a creative, fall-themed basemap style.

The Right to Live | The Right to Work

The history of anti-labor "Right to Work" laws in the United States, showing their spread and consequences.

Footsteps of La Crosse

Maps for a series of historical walking tours created for the La Crosse Public Library archives.

Voices of La Crosse

A series of maps connected thematically related sites of the Hear, Here public history project created by the Public Library in La Crosse, Wisconsin.

Atlas of the Pacific Northwest

The first online edition of the long-published Atlas of the Pacific Northwest, a compilation of maps and data centered on Oregon, Washington, and Idaho. Created in partnership with Oregon State University Libraries and Press.

Oregon Water Atlas

An atlas compiling a variety of data and stories about water in the state of Oregon. Created in partnership with the Institute for Water and Watersheds for my undergraduate thesis.

Global Refugee Atlas

An atlas highlighting the extent and conditions of refugee camps and settlements across the world. Created in partnership with Dr. Bo Zhao and Giovanni Svevo.

North American Cryptid Map

A story map about lake monsters and bigfoot sightings. My first web map!

Sandy River Shuttle Map

Access points and recreation areas along the Sandy River in East Multnomah County, Oregon, created with Matthew Hampton for Oregon Metro.

Atlas of the Polar Regions

An iBooks atlas showing a variety of Arctic and Antarctic topics. Created for a computer-assisted cartography class taught by Drs. Bernhard Jenny and Jane Darbyshire.
