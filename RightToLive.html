The Right To Live | The Right to Work

Labor statistics are often inherently spatial, yet rarely are they geographically visualized. This project shows labor union data at the ssate level to highlight disparities between Right to Work states and states with tighter union protections. To do this, I physically split the country into two distinct entities, and also used a different typeface for each grouping. Obviously, this map is only a first step, but it could serve as a building block for future labor visualization. One aspect missing from this map is the segregationist logic of early Right-to-Work discourse, as well the as role Right-to-Work laws play in minimizing the power of strikes.

Gareth Baldrica-Franklin, Mapmaker baldricafran@wisc.edu
